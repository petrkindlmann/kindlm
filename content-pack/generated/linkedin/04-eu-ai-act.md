# LinkedIn Post: EU AI Act Compliance

---

August 2026 is closer than you think.

That's when the EU AI Act's Annex IV requirements take full effect for high-risk AI systems. If your AI agent processes insurance claims, screens job applicants, assists in medical decisions, or handles financial advisory — you're likely in scope.

Annex IV requires documented testing evidence: methodologies, metrics, results, robustness testing, and risk mitigation. Not a checkbox. Quantitative evidence with statistical significance.

Here's what many teams don't realize: if you're already doing behavioral testing properly, you have most of what you need. The gap isn't in the testing — it's in the documentation.

Every test run you execute today with proper logging generates timestamped, version-controlled evidence of systematic testing. Every assertion result is a data point in your compliance documentation. Every baseline comparison shows behavioral stability over time.

KindLM can generate Annex IV-compliant reports directly from your test results:

```bash
kindlm test --compliance
```

The output maps your behavioral test results to specific regulatory requirements: testing methodologies, safety guardrail validation, PII detection evidence, statistical analysis across multiple runs, and SHA-256 hashes for tamper evidence.

This isn't a substitute for legal counsel. But it transforms compliance from a separate workstream into a natural output of good engineering practice.

The best time to start building compliance documentation was when the AI Act was published. The second best time is now.
